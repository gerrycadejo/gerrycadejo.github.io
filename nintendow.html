Nintendo
Wavemode

Type: Future Lions 2019 entry
Brief: Connect a brand with an audience in a way that wasn't possible 3 years ago.
Idea: We combined VR and mind gaming in order to take preventive action against epilepsy in children.
Team: Nitin George
Role: Research and concept development.

In general, because I'm keen to work with projects that have an impact on people's life. But more concretely, I'm showing this case because of the way we manage to simplify a very complex subject and the decisions that we took in order to define our scope.

To solve the brief, we opted to have our own version of a tech-first approach. Meaning that we first chose a product and then we thought about the opportunities that were present in its related field or outside of it.

We agreed to go with Nintendo Labo and tackle video game epilepsy. We tried different angles and we raised questions such as, should we focus on preventive or reactive behavior? Or where are we aiming? Helping patients with their symptoms or physicians with the treatment?

Questions kept popping up, therefore we decided to frame the brief around one of the most urgent subjects of epilepsy: silent symptoms. We asked ourselves:

How might we enable early detection of epilepsy in children?

The first half of that answer, came to us after researching about EEG sensors and how they are being used in mind gaming. Specifically, it came to us after finding that EEG sensors are also used in brain scans for epilepsy.

Here is the other half of our solution:

  • I believe our idea was preventive at its core, in a way that involved the main actors around the subject and it cater to their specific needs: Kids could enjoy their games, parents gained more control over an unpredictable matter and physicians could finally have a starting point to work with.
  • From a brand perspective, perhaps this was not the smartest move, in a sense that Nintendo would take a lot of risk for an uncertain reward. Even more, there's the chance that parents would end their cycle as Nintendo's customers.
  • Also, I believe we lacked information about how mind gaming happens and how to implement this hybrid type of game flow in a seamless way.
