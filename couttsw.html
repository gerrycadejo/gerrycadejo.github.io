Coutts
Wealthness

Type: D&AD New blood Awards entry
Brief: Connect with a new breed of wealthy clients through emerging forms of technology.
Idea: We created an app that displays customized financial content tailored for workouts and triggered by movement.
Team: Nitin George
Role: UX and creative strategy.

This project had a big learning curve for me in terms of understanding the brand and its target audience. Also, this project required me to trust my gut when facing an overload of information and the need to "play it safe".

Neither of us knew much about private banking, therefore we started by defining the key interactions between account managers and their clients, and mapping the process considering clients' expectations and Coutts UVP.

After that, we continued our research on who our target was. We went through different yearly reports on young high net worth individuals (HNWI) in order to understand their current feelings and needs when dealing with their finances.

These were our three main findings:

  • Young HNWI are more focused on wealth creation than in wealth preservation.
  • They need guidance and prefer a hybrid financial advice model.
  • They work out as often as possiible.

With this information we were able to formulate our question:

How might we slide into the young HNWI workouts to empower them into making financial decisions?

We answered it by combining current trends on wearable devices with their need for customized advice. We answered it on this way:

  • Our solution enabled Coutts' clients to gain more control and select financial areas that resonate more with who they are, in a way that is interactive and non-intrusive. With our solution, we created a small space for Coutts into their clients daily routine.
  • Looking back I believe that we delivered a partial solution, it lacked a final nudge that could make HNWI take more financial decisions and therefore provide a tangible benefit for Coutts.
  • Even though we piggybacked onto an existing habit, we had no clue on how willing they were to exchange their Spotify playlist for a blockchain article.
