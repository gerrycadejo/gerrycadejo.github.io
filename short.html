by Gerardo Gómez

Yep, that's me!

When I was 17 my plans for the future were simple. Start a respected career, earn big money and get a "well done mijo" every now and then.

It was a simple and proven process. My father did it, my grandma did it as well, and my gf was going to do it too. It was so simple and yet I was feeling stupid because of having second thoughts.

I didn't know what to do, the norm was: first find a job and then try to find what you were passionate about. There were no other choices and definitely, there were no companies, such as ICA, willing to prove that there's always a better way.

Eventually, it was time to make a decision and I decided to go to med school. It felt great telling others about what I was studying and I looked A-M-A-Z-I-N-G wearing white scrubs.

Only now, I'm able to understand that I made that decision because it was a way for me to fulfill my need to make the World a better place. A need that's is still present with me at every workday and with every brief. A need that guided me through when I was working with the Nintendo Labo brief.

Med school is not for everyone and certainly, it was not for me. As much as I liked the idea of becoming this "well-respected doctor", I could not find the joy in it.

Back then, I was afraid I sounded childish when I was explaining to others how important it was for me to feel passionate about what I was studying. Looking back, I'm happy with my decision. Nowadays, I love what I do and I go to bed with a foolish smile all over my face. At least, most of the days. For instance, the days I worked with: Apple tv.

After med school, I went with marketing. It felt heartless in comparison to being a doctor, but as I keep learning more about it, I really liked how it enabled me to do the things that I loved. Especially, I liked the fact that being a generalist was seen as a quality instead of a handicap.

Once I started working, I realized how ignorant I was about... well everything. Nonetheless, I also learned that if having experience is what make things easier, it is passion what makes things possible. Nowadays, I'm still ignorant but I think I 've improved in how I deal with the unknown. Exhibit A: Coutts.

On my last semester of my bachelor's degree, I was introduced to the power of ideas and its potential to last and inspire generation after generation.

What's more, I was so thrilled about this realization that I started looking for jobs that had "working with ideas" at the core of the role. Needless to say, it was love at first sight when I stumbled with the ad industry.

Over the last years I've worked at different agencies and in all of them, I've been lucky enough to have mentors around me. They were all really patient, and they all shared the need to go beyond the obvious and always strive for the big idea. It is because of my mentors that I was able to be part of this type of work:Chamba Billboards.

Life in the tropics is great and Guatemala is not the exception. Working there was fantastic and it left me with many lessons, but I knew that in order to expand my horizons I needed to fly away from my nest.

For this reason, I moved to Sweden to study at Hyper Island.

Moving to Sweden was not as hard as I thought it would be. Swedish people are nice and Stockholm is a beautiful city.

Nonetheless, I was shocked with the price of beers. Something needed to be done! Therefore, with courage, the Rolling Stones playing in the background and my basic understanding of coding, I created: Priceless bars.

I started studying at Hyper Island a year ago. Here, is where I've increased my list of "things I know I don't know" and where I've pushed my limits. It is now, on my last year at Hyper Island, where my plot is currently unraveling.

Funnily enough, 10 years ago I was in a similar position to the one I'm today. Finishing my studies and defining my future steps. Even though I feel more prepared than how I did back then, I feel very excited to find out what would my my next chapter is going to be about.
