ICA
Hackathon

Type: Schoolwork.
Brief: Become the most attractive employer for potential employees
Idea: We repurposed an interactive video into an application process based on our target's passions.
Team: Samira Gomes, Aimee Dean, Nitin George, Ellen Forsberg and Nico Ruponen.
Role: Creative strategy and co-writer of the story.

With this project, I'm very happy about the planning process that we had and how we pushed ourselves to go from a simple insight into an unorthodox solution. Also, this project combines the three areas I'm more keen to work with: tech, stories, and strategy. Not to mention that we won the hackathon.

ICA offers jobs for all kind of people at different stages of their life and while this is good for their diversity goals, it represented a challenge for us since we could've not met all of their audience's needs. Therefore, our first decision was to only target students looking for an entry-level position.

Once we settled that piece, we defined our question:

How might we help ICA become the most attractive employer for our target group?

After some research and discussion, we discovered that the usual recruitment process is filled with confusing job titles and general roles descriptions, making it hard to understand if this job offer is aligned with the things you are passionate about

We had our insight and we knew how hard ICA works to have a culture of professional growth and development, that's why we created ICAN. An application process camouflaged as an interactive video that portraits ICA as the place where you CAN have a meaningful career based on the things that you care for.

  • Our solution disrupted the norm of application processes, where instead of choosing a job you were selecting the things you enjoy. The format we'd chosen allowed us to convey how easy it was to do what you love in a casual day at ICA and how their work culture manifests itself in even the smallest interaction.
  • Now, I think our solution had a short lifespan and it's hard to scale, perhaps it was smarter to separate it from their website and use it only as a gimmick at job fairs or at school visits.
