1

I used to write plays when I was in high school. I remember writing for hours, reading it out loud, editing, laughing and then fantasizing about the opening show. It was really fun and I was just a boy. And like any teenager, I was oblivious of what I could do or what I was wishful to do as a grown-up.

I liked telling jokes, I liked unleashing my stupidity, but I knew nothing about creativity as a career path. I was all about the fancy job titles, academic achievements and serious adults matters.

I was just a boy, completely unaware that 10+ years later I was gonna trade the academic life for free-spirited experiences and have "telling stories" as one of my main skills.

I couldn't have guessed that a day like today I would call myself a creative and do serious adult kind of work without losing my own stupid touch. For example Apple TV Bites.

2

When it was time to make a call, I decided to go to med school. Partly because I grew up watching George Clooney as a doctor, but mainly cause I believed that only then I could change the world.

I had the drive and it lasted until I encountered the wonders and horrors of the human body. Needless to say, it was the end of paging dr. Gerry.

After med school, I tried a different approach and I chose to do something I kind of liked, in this case creating videos. So I went with marketing.

At the beginning it felt a little bit heartless, using words like "suppliers" and reading Kotler's books. However, as I dived deeper into it, I was introduced to the power of ideas and how they can change a whole society. Until today, I still get thrilled by thinking of this, I still have a strong impulse to do good, to create for a greater cause. I have an impulse to change what I think needs to be changed: exhibit A Nintendo Labo.

3

Time passed and I finished my bachelor in Marketing. Even then I could tell that I've made the best decision I could. I enjoyed working with it, I had a purpose to work with it and honestly I did not suck at it.

Especially, I liked the fact that it allowed me to be my fullest version of myself. Everything I've lived before, the things I've cared for, the shows I've watched, it all helped me to become a better professional, to take my peculiarity and from it, connect with the world.

People who knew me at my early stages, could often described me as an idiot on fire. I had no clue about what I was doing but I had the will to make things work and make them good.

Today I'd like to think people's perception of me has changed, even though I don't mind having this "idiot" aura around me. I mean, every new brief it's a learning process, where if you are curious enough you'll get yourself caught up in flames and after a few errors, you'll no longer be an idiot(at least until the next brief comes around). That's how I felt with this project: Coutts.

4

It's always been hard to explain what I do to friends and family. It was hard when I was a media planner, a little less confusing as a copywriter and too damn abstract when I worked as a digital creative.

Also, even if they had a more or less clear idea about what I did, I had this constant suspicion that they all assume none of these roles involve hard work.

I can't blame them, where I come from not only would you find ads everywhere, but 90% of them might kill you if you stare for too long. Most of these, are packed with lame puns and flashy colors, like how hard could it be to create them?

What they're missing is the other 10% of the work, the one that sticks, the one that brings some sunlight into one's lonely soul. I was lucky enough to have mentors that guided me towards striving to create this type of work. Moreover, they even trusted me to have the resilience to be part of their projects. For instance: Chamba Billboards.

5

My story would not be complete without telling you about my home country: Guatemala. I've lived there for almost my whole life and I've formed my identity around loud noises, warm people, tropic weather and never-ending surrealism.

There, the reality is harsh and negativity is not an option, therefore kindness is at every corner. You'll find it when strangers greet you inside a lift, when food keeps filling your plate (especially after you're done) and in the eyes of people you accidentally meet while walking down the streets.

Moving to Stockholm was not as hard as I thought it would be. Fika is great, jalapenos are cheap and I've met lovely people whom I truly admire.

Though during my first weeks here, I thought of swimming my way back to Guatemala after checking out the prices of beers. As I said before, I like creating things that contribute to the overall wellbeing of humankind, so with that in mind, I created this: Priceless bars.

6

Hyper Island is the reason why I'm living in Stockholm. Here I found my new creative outlet with coding, learned about processes that foster creativity and failed graciously in projects with outstanding people by my side. It's at Hyper, that I feel almost in the same place I was 10+ years ago, cracking up my future and excited about what's next.

This is as far as my story gets, but how about some spoilers? Right now I'd love to keep working with ideas, in the role of a creative technologist.

Although, any other title will do. As long as I have: tech, stories, and strategy at the core of what I do, It'll be alright. I mean, my folks won't know what I do anyway.
