1

I used to write plays when I was in high school. I remember writing for hours, reading it out loud, editing, laughing and then fantasizing about the opening show. Even though, me and my plays got butchered everytime by the student publication critique (Damn you Pablo!), it was really fun and those were my firsts tries as a writer.

Back then, all I was certain of was that I liked being stupid and me and my highschool sweetheart were gonna stay together forever. I was 17 at the time, the world was mine and failure was not even on my radar.

I was all about the fancy job titles, academic achievements and serious adults matters. Now I see, I was just a boy, completely unaware that 10+ years later I was gonna trade the academic life for free-spirited experiences and have "telling stories" as one of my main skills.

I couldn't have possibly guessed that a day like today I would call myself a creative and do serious adult kind of work without losing my own stupid touch. For example Apple TV Bites.

2

After giving it no thought and discussing it with everyone, I decided to go to med school. Partly because I grew up watching George Clooney as a doctor, but mainly cause I believed that only then I could change the world.

I had the drive to make it happen and my mother made sure I had the whitest doctor's coat ever. They both remained neat, until the day I encountered the wonders and horrors of a real human body. Needless to say, it was the end of paging dr. Gerry.

After med school, I tried a different approach and I chose to do something I kind of liked, in this case creating videos. So I went with marketing.

At the beginning it felt a little bit heartless, using words like "suppliers" and reading Kotler's books. However, as I dived deeper into it, I was introduced to the power of ideas and how they can change a whole society. Until today, I still get thrilled by thinking of this, I still have a strong impulse to do good, to create for a greater cause. I have an impulse to change what I think needs to be changed: exhibit A Nintendo Labo.

3

Time passed and I finished my bachelor in Marketing. Even then I could tell that I'd made a very good decision. I enjoyed working with it, I had a purpose to work with it and honestly I did not suck at it.

Especially, I liked the fact that it allowed me to be my fullest version of myself. Everything I've lived before, the things that I've cared for, the shows that I've watched, it all helped me become a better professional, to take my peculiarity and from it, connect with the world.

People who knew me at my early stages, could often described me as an idiot on fire, due to my overall lack of understanding and my eagerness to make things work and make them work good.

Today, I know more than I did before and I think people's perception of me has changed, even though I don't mind having this tag around me. Sometimes you start an expert, others you don't. That's business and there are many beginnings in between briefs, brands and industries.

It's a learning process, where curiousity will get yourself up in flames and help you endure/amend error after error, until you'll no longer an idiot (at least, until the next starting line shows up). That's how I felt with this project: Coutts.

4

It's always been hard to explain what I do to friends and family. It was hard when I was a media planner, a little less confusing as a copywriter and too damn abstract when I worked as a digital creative.

Also, even if they had a more or less clear idea about what I did, I had this constant suspicion that they all assume none of these roles involve hard work.

I can't blame them, where I come from not only would you find ads everywhere, but 90% of them might kill you if you stare for too long. Most of these, are packed with lame puns and flashy colors, like how hard could it be to create them?

The problem is, they are neglecting the process and even worst they're missing the other 10% of the work. The great work, the "it made me cry" kind of work, the one that brings some sunlight into one's lonely soul. Thankfully, I was lucky enough to have mentors that guided me into always strive to create this type of work. Moreover, they even trusted me to have the resilience to be part of their projects. For instance: Chamba Billboards.

5

My story would not be complete without telling you about my home country: Guatemala. I've lived there for almost my whole life and I've formed my identity around loud noises, warm people, tropic weather and never-ending surrealism.

There, the reality is harsh and negativity is not an option, therefore kindness is at every corner. You'll find it when strangers greet you inside a lift, when food keeps filling your plate (especially after you're done) and in the eyes of people you accidentally meet while walking down the streets.

Nonetheless, as much as I loved my yard, moving to Stockholm was not as hard as I thought it would be. Fika is great, jalapenos are cheap and I've met lovely people whom I truly admire.

Though, I need to say that during my first weeks here and checking out the beer prices, I thought of swimming my way back to Guatemala. I love beer and as I said before, I like creating things that contribute to the overall wellbeing of humankind, so with that in mind, I created this: Priceless bars.

6

Hyper Island is the reason why I'm living in Stockholm. Here I found a different side of my passions, increased the list of subjects "I know I don't know" and failed graciously in projects with outstanding people by my side. It's at Hyper, that I feel almost in the same place that I was 10+ years ago. Restless and busy cracking up my future, but overall excited about what's coming next.

Looking back, I'm grateful for my journey and its invaluable lessons. Even though I have not learned how to foreseen the future (yet), I can easily see myself immersed in ideas and finding solutions with a 10k HD smile on my face.

Hopefully, on my next chapter you can read that I'm working with tech, stories and strategy. Perhaps and I'm crossing fingers, my next chapter starts with the time you helped me land an intership as a creative technologist.
