Apple
TV Bites

Type: Future Lions 2019 entry
Brief: Connect a brand with an audience in a way that wasn't possible 3 years ago.
Idea: We added a food recommendation feature, based on the shows that you see, to the Apple TV
Team: Nitin George
Role: Creative strategy and copywriting.

Why am I showing this?

This project was made by intuition and google searches. It challenged me to fill my knowledge gap with imagination and stubbornness. It was a process of thinking, testing, scratching and starting again.

The Process

For this project, we kind of needed to craft our own brief and Nitin and I decided to put a pin on saving the world and instead we tried finding opportunities within everyday problems. That's why we went with a common burden of ours, how to decide what to eat?

We discovered that it's easier to make decisions when you have been craving a meal and we both agreed that the biggest craving factory out there was Netflix.

It was a silly insight(ish) but still it helped us frame our main question:

What if we can make easier food-related-decisions by watching more shows?

We realized that there were new playes in the streaming industry and that a physical deviced would make things easier. With this in mind, we went with Apple TV instead of Netflix.

We did the simplest user journey we could think of and after a while, we selected computer vision and eye tracking technology as our main ingredients. We had something, but our recipee was still missing accuracy and it stayed like that, until the vital radio technology came in.

The Idea

Why we went with this idea

  • We liked this idea because even though it sounded extremely complicated it always felt feasible. The business opportunities around Apple's streaming service and the Apple Wallet encouraged us to continue until the end. Also, it had that vibe of practically using magic to solve a common problem.

What I would've done differently

  • I think that we focused too much on new technologies instead of going for a more elegant solution with the existing Apple products. For instance, was it easier to use the Apple Watch's ECG instead of vital radio waves?
  • Also, I think the case study could have gone beyond the binge-watching moment and explored new interactions. (i.e. Asking Siri your cravings at a food court)
